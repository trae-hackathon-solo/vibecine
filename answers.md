## Creation Story *

We built VibeCine for the PixVerse hackathon because one long text-to-video prompt is painful to control: scenes feel disconnected, the character drifts between shots, and a single bad clip forces you to regenerate everything.

VibeCine treats each React Flow node as one **shot**, not one frame. You upload a character reference image, describe your story, and TRAE generates a 3–5 shot storyboard you can edit in place. Each shot gets its own PixVerse clip—regenerate only what failed—then merge completed clips into one final MP4. The goal is a judge-friendly, end-to-end demo: story in, editable shots, video out.

## Team Introduction *

We are a solo hackathon team building for the PixVerse track. Our focus is shipping a complete, demo-ready workflow—not production infrastructure—so judges can see the full path from story prompt to final video in one session.

We used TRAE to move fast on storyboard generation and prompt iteration, and PixVerse for per-shot video generation. The split of roles was simple: product flow and React Flow UI, Next.js API routes and PixVerse CLI integration, and ffmpeg-based clip merging for the final output. VibeCine is our answer to “how do you control a multi-shot AI film when one prompt is not enough?”
